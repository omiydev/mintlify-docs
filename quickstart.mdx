This guide walks you through your first end-to-end analysis on Galagos. You will create a free account, upload a file, describe your research question in plain English, and receive a structured report with visualizations — all without writing a single line of code.

<Tip>
  The free Individual plan includes 20 one-time credits, which is enough to run several complete analyses. No credit card is required to sign up.
</Tip>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a free account">
    Go to [galagos.ai/register](https://galagos.ai/register) and sign up with your email address or Google account. No credit card is required for the free Individual plan.

    Once you confirm your email, you will land on the Galagos dashboard.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create your first project">
    From the dashboard, click **New project** and give it a name that reflects your dataset or research question — for example, "RNA-seq pilot study".

    A project is the persistent workspace that holds all of your files, analyses, and outputs together. You can have up to 5 active projects on the free plan.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload a file">
    Inside your project, click **Upload files** and select your data. Galagos accepts the following formats natively:

    | Format | Typical use case |
    |--------|-----------------|
    | FASTQ  | Raw sequencing reads |
    | VCF    | Variant call data |
    | BAM    | Aligned sequencing reads |
    | H5AD   | Single-cell expression data |
    | CSV    | Tabular data and metadata |

    <Note>
      Files are uploaded into your project's isolated storage. They are never used to train models or shared outside your workspace.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Describe your research question">
    Once your file is uploaded, type your question into the analysis prompt. Write it the same way you would explain the task to a colleague — plain English works best.

    A concrete example:

    > Perform differential gene expression analysis comparing treatment vs control groups. Use DESeq2 and generate a volcano plot highlighting genes with adjusted p-value below 0.05 and log2 fold change greater than 1.

    Galagos reads your question, writes a plain-English plan in the right panel before compute starts, selects the appropriate tools from its library of 250+ pre-built Skills, and runs the analysis end-to-end.

    <Info>
      You do not need to name specific tools unless you have a preference. Galagos selects the right methods based on your file type and question.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review your results">
    When the analysis completes, Galagos presents a structured report that includes:

    - **Visualizations** — volcano plots, heatmaps, bar charts, and other figures appropriate to your analysis
    - **Summary findings** — a written interpretation of the key results
    - **Literature-grounded context** — supporting evidence pulled from PubMed, UniProt, and NCBI
    - **Audit trail** — every step, tool, and parameter used in the workflow

    If a step encounters an error, Galagos detects the failure and attempts an alternative approach automatically before surfacing a result.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Download or share your results">
    From the results view, click **Download report** to export the full report and figures. You can also share a project link with collaborators who have a Galagos account.

    Every project preserves its context, steps, and outputs so the analysis is fully reproducible — by you or anyone you share it with.
  </Step>
</Steps>
