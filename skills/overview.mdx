A Skill is a short note, in plain English, that tells the agent how you want a particular kind of work done. The agent reads attached Skills before running and treats them as guidance — closer to a brief for a new lab member than to code.

Galagos ships with a catalogue of **250+ prebuilt Skills** covering the most common bioinformatics tools and workflows. You attach a handful per project, toggle them on or off as you go, and write your own when the catalogue doesn't quite fit.

## Per-project, toggleable

Skills live at the project level. The wrench icon in a project's right sidebar opens the Skills panel, which lists every Skill attached to that project with a toggle next to each. The panel header shows how many are enabled out of the total (for example, *"4 of 7 enabled"*).

Switching a toggle takes effect on the next prompt. When the agent uses a Skill, it appears as a step in the Steps list — visible proof the toggle worked.

<Tip>
  Start with two or three Skills per project, not twenty. Focused guidance produces better results than a long list of competing instructions.
</Tip>

## Adding from the catalogue

Click **+ Add** in the Skills panel to open the catalogue. The catalogue:

- Search live-filters as you type — typing *"alpha"* surfaces AlphaFold, AlphaFold-Database, alphagenome.
- Cards show the Skill name and a one-line description. Click any card for the full text.
- Categories group Skills by domain: Genomics, Single-Cell, Drug Discovery, Protein Structure, Proteomics, Chemistry, Clinical, and more.

## Save a conversation as a Skill

After a successful run, you can convert the conversation itself into a reusable Skill. Two routes:

- **From a suggestion** — after a handful of completed responses in a session, the agent often offers *"Save this as a Skill?"*. Click it to capture the workflow.
- **From the Skills panel** — click **Create**. Describe what you want the Skill to do in plain English; the agent drafts it from the recent conversation and asks you to confirm.

Saved Skills land in **My Skills**, your personal library. They're available from every project you create.

## Workspace-level Skills

In a team workspace, owners and admins can configure Skills for the whole team:

- **Pinned** Skills are pre-attached when a member creates a new project.
- **Required** Skills cannot be toggled off — useful for non-negotiable QC thresholds, citation rules, or privacy policies.

Members can always attach additional Skills inside their project. See [Workspaces](/concepts/workspaces) for the full picture.

## Custom Skills you write yourself

If a workflow doesn't quite fit the catalogue, write a Skill in plain English:

- *"Whenever I upload gene expression data, log-transform counts, filter out genes with zero reads in more than half the samples, and include both fold change and adjusted p-value in the output."*
- *"For every phylogenetic tree, root on the outgroup I specify, colour branches by host species, and scale by substitutions per site."*

The agent saves your text as a Skill in the project. **Save to My Skills** promotes it to your personal library.

## Sharing Skills

Skills can be exported as a small file and imported into another workspace or shared with a colleague. Shared team libraries are on the way for teams that want a single curated set across many workspaces.

## A few tips

- **Brief like a new lab member, not a computer.** Tell the Skill *why* and *when*, not just *what*.
- **One sentence is enough to start.** Refine after the first run shows you what's missing.
- **Update Skills when your approach changes.** Six months later, your defaults may not match what you wrote.
- **Don't over-attach.** Two or three Skills per project beats ten that pull in different directions.
