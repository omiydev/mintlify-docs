Credits are the unit of consumption for AI-powered analysis in Galagos. Each time Galagos makes an inference call — to plan a workflow, run a skill, or interpret results — it draws from your credit balance. Storage is separate and is measured per project, not per account. Understanding both helps you plan your work and choose the right plan.

## Credits

### What consumes a credit

A credit is spent each time Galagos calls the underlying language model. In practice, this happens when you:

- Send a new analysis request
- Ask Galagos to re-plan or revise a workflow
- Request a summary or interpretation of results

Simple follow-up questions within an ongoing analysis typically consume fewer credits than starting a new, complex workflow from scratch.

### Credit allotments by plan

| Plan             | Price       | Credits included         |
| ---------------- | ----------- | ------------------------ |
| Individual       | Free (beta) | 20 credits (one-time)    |
| Starter          | €35/month   | 50 credits/month         |
| Professional     | €150/month  | 200 credits/month        |
| Enterprise       | Custom      | Pooled compute credits   |

<Warning>
  Credits do not roll over to the next billing period. Any unused credits at the end of the month are forfeited. Plan your analyses accordingly — or upgrade if you consistently run out before renewal.
</Warning>

### What happens when you run out of credits

If your balance reaches zero mid-analysis, the current job stops. Galagos saves the state of the workflow so you do not lose progress, but no new inference calls are made until your balance is restored.

To resume, you can:

- **Wait for your monthly renewal** — credits reset at the start of your next billing cycle
- **Upgrade your plan** — a higher plan tier gives you a larger monthly allotment immediately

### Tips for using credits efficiently

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Be specific in your prompts">
    A precise request — "run DESeq2 on my two-condition RNA-seq data with default parameters and produce a volcano plot" — requires fewer planning iterations than a vague one like "analyze my data." The more context you give upfront, the less back-and-forth Galagos needs to clarify your intent.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use pre-built skills instead of open-ended requests">
    Pre-built skills have their parameters and pipeline steps pre-defined. When you invoke a skill directly (e.g., "run the STAR alignment skill on sample_1.fastq"), Galagos skips the planning phase and goes straight to execution, saving credits that would otherwise be spent on workflow design.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Batch your questions">
    If you have several questions about the same dataset, ask them together in a single prompt rather than as separate messages. Galagos can address multiple sub-tasks in one inference pass.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Review results before re-running">
    Check the output of each step before asking Galagos to continue. Catching a parameter mistake early — before a long downstream pipeline runs — avoids consuming credits on work you will discard.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Storage

### How storage is measured

Storage is measured per project. Each project on your account has its own quota — uploading a 5 GB file to project A does not affect project B's available space.

### Storage limits by plan

| Plan           | Storage per project |
| -------------- | ------------------- |
| Individual     | 10 GB               |
| Starter        | 25 GB               |
| Professional   | 100 GB              |
| Enterprise     | Custom              |

### Managing storage

<Tip>
  Intermediate files — such as sorted BAM files produced during alignment — are often the largest outputs and are rarely needed after downstream analysis completes. Deleting them once you have confirmed your results is the fastest way to recover space.
</Tip>

To see what is using your project's storage, open the project, click **Files**, and sort by size. You can delete individual files or bulk-select them from that view.

If you need more storage than your current plan allows, upgrading your plan increases the per-project quota across all your projects.
